1 · The 5 roles — who is who

Worker · मज़दूर

Works at the site. Can only see his own info, and can request an expense when he spends money for the site.

Driver · ड्राइवर

Runs one vehicle at a time. Fills a morning and evening vehicle update every day, with photos.

Team Head · मिस्त्री

Leads a team of workers. Enters the team's daily expenses and the site progress report.

Site Manager · साइट मैनेजर

In charge of one site. Approves requests, manages people and vehicles, controls the settings.

Owner · मालिक

You. See everything, from every site — every rupee, every photo, every approval.

2 · The golden rule — who sees whose data

Data always flows UP, never sideways. Everyone fills only their own work; the people above them can see it.

Note: the Driver reports directly to the Site Manager (not to a Team Head).

3 · Worker · मज़दूर The simplest screen in the app — view + one form only

W-1 His home screen

When a worker opens the app he sees:

  • His ID card — name, his site, his phone number.
  • His expense requests and their status: APPROVED REJECTED (with reason) or waiting.

W-2 Emergency numbers — one tap to call

At the bottom of his screen, always visible:

  • His Site Manager and Team Head — added automatically.
  • Police · Ambulance · Hospital · Fire · Site office — the Site Manager fills these numbers once for the site (see S-7).

Tap a name → phone dials it. Useful in any emergency at the site.

W-3 The Expense Request form — the only form he fills

When a worker spends his own money for the site (buys something from a shop), he fills this. It does not count as money spent yet — it becomes an expense only after his Team Head or Site Manager approves it.

Form fields & rules
1
Amount ₹ requiredNumbers only. Must be within the limit you set (e.g. ₹2,000 — see S-7). Above the limit, the form stops and tells him: "ask your Team Head / Site Manager".
2
Date autoToday's date fills automatically. He may pick today or up to 2 days back (a 3-day window). Future dates are not allowed.
3
Category requiredPicks one from a list — Food, Material, Transport, Repair… You control which categories appear and their names (see S-7).
4
Bill photoCamera or gallery. The main proof. Strongly encouraged.
5
More photos (optional)Up to 2–3 extra photos.
6
Remark (optional)A short note in his words.
7
Voice note (optional)Press and speak — for those who don't like typing. The approver hears it before deciding.
Who does what with this form WORKERfills it TEAM HEADapproves / rejects SITE MANAGERapproves / rejects OWNERsees it DRIVER — cannot see

After approval, the expense stays permanently visible to the Team Head and Site Manager in the site's expense history.

W-4 What a worker can NOT do

  • Cannot see any other person's data or the site's total expenses.
  • Cannot add an expense directly — only request it.
  • No attendance in the app — attendance stays in your manual register for now.

T-4 Approvals — his workers' expense requests

Every request from his workers lands here with the amount, photos, voice note and remark — he can decide without phoning anyone. He can correct the category before approving. Rejecting requires a reason, which the worker sees.

T-5 People

  • Sees his team's active members.
  • Can add a new person to his team and set his role.
  • CANNOT REMOVE anyone — removing/deactivating a person is Site-Manager-only (S-6).

T-6 Not for the Team Head (this phase)

6 · Site Manager · साइट मैनेजर The site's command center — and its settings

S-1 Day-wise dashboard — pick a date, see everything

He picks any date and sees that day's full picture of his site, each in its own block:

  • All progress reports of that day (photos + voice, right there).
  • All expenses of that day — and who entered each one.
  • All requests of that day — who raised it, who approved it, what happened.
  • Totals for Today / This week / This month.
  • Quick views: Yesterday · day before · any day of the last 7 days · last 30 days · or pick any date from the calendar.

Two levels: the dashboard shows the quick summary; a separate, full Insights page goes deeper — choose a day and everything of that day opens up: every worker's entries, the complete expense history, every request and its outcome.

S-2 Expense entry — counts instantly, with one guard-rail

Same form as T-2, with no date restriction. Entries are booked immediately — and you (Owner) see every one.

Up to ₹1,00,000 per entrybooked instantly — no approval (three bills of ₹50,000 in one day are fine)
Above ₹1,00,000 in one entrygoes to YOU (Owner) as a request — counted only after your approval

You (Owner) can change the Site Manager's limit anytime.

S-3 Progress report

The same form as the Team Head's (T-3). Either of them filing covers the day for the site.

S-4 Approval center — everything comes to him

  • Expense requests — from workers, drivers, and the Team Head's older-than-7-days entries. On approval he confirms or corrects the category, then the money is counted.
  • Vehicle change requests from drivers.
  • Damage reports — he reviews and closes them.

A person can never approve his own request — the app blocks it.

S-5 Vehicles page — tap anything, get its full story

  • Register a new vehicle — number, type, name, and 1–2 photos of the vehicle so every machine is recognizable at a glance.
  • Tap a vehicle → its full history: meter readings, diesel taken (litres + ₹), total money spent on it, trips/loads, and every damage with photos and voice notes.
  • Tap a driver → everything by that driver: daily updates, fuel, trips, expenses.

Vehicle analytics — because the driver logs readings daily (D-2/D-3), each vehicle's page shows simple numbers that tell its truth. Visible to the Site Manager and you:

  • Average running per day — last 7 / 30 / 90 days (km or hours).
  • Diesel/petrol used, and the vehicle's monthly running cost.
  • No vehicle-vs-vehicle comparison for now (as you said) — deeper analytics can grow later.

S-6 People — full control

  • Adds Team Heads, Drivers, Workers to his site.
  • Only he can deactivate (remove) a person.

S-8 The one-site rule

A Site Manager belongs to exactly one site. He can never see another site's people, money, vehicles or reports — not even their names. Each site is a sealed box; only you see all the boxes.

  • Every vehicle and driver — same drill-down pages the Site Manager has, but across all sites.
  • Every request with its full trail: who raised it → who approved it → what it became.
  • Day / week / month views — per site, per person, per vehicle.

O-2 Reports & company defaults

  • Download Excel reports of the data.
  • Set company-wide defaults for the settings in S-7 — each site can then adjust its own.

9 · Money ledger — who holds how much ★ NEW — added from your feedback. This is how the advance money (petty cash) gets tracked.

M-1 Giving money is recorded in the app

Whenever money is handed down, the giver records it in ten seconds: amount · to whom · date · note.

Owner gives ₹1,00,000 to the Site Managerrecorded → the Site Manager's balance becomes ₹1,00,000
he distributes
Site Manager gives ₹5,000 to the Team Head · ₹500 to a worker…each one recorded → their balances go up, his goes down

Money can come back UP too: if someone is going on leave or the work ends, he returns his remaining balance — the return is recorded the same way, and both balances adjust automatically.

M-2 Every approved expense cuts the spender's balance

When a worker's ₹300 expense is approved, ₹300 is deducted from his balance automatically. When the Team Head enters today's kirana spend, it cuts his balance. Nothing to calculate by hand.

Balance = money received − approved expenses. That simple.

M-3 Everyone sees his own khata

Each person's screen shows three numbers: received · spent · balance left — so there is never an argument about "how much did I give you last week?"

M-4 Your answer to "where did my one lakh go?"

Your dashboard shows, for every rupee you sent down, who used it and on what. Example:

  • ₹50,000 — diesel & vehicles
  • ₹20,000 — workers' daily needs & site maintenance
  • ₹18,000 — material & repairs
  • ₹12,000 — still with the Site Manager (his balance)
Who does what OWNERgives money · sees all khatas SITE MANAGERgives money · own khata TEAM HEADown khata WORKERown khata DRIVERown khata

Two questions for you (quote M-4): ① When money is handed over, should the receiver also tap "yes, received" in the app? ② If a worker's balance is ₹200, should the app stop a ₹500 request — or allow it and show minus?

10 · Shops & vendor accounts (udhaar khata) ★ NEW — promoted from SUG-3. You asked for a proper flow; here it is.

V-1 The site's shop list

The Site Manager saves the regular shops once: shop name · phone · what it sells (hardware, kirana, diesel pump…). Most site purchases happen at these few shops. Every site keeps its own local shop list.

V-2 Buying on credit — no cash leaves anyone's pocket

Anyone — worker, driver, Team Head, even the Site Manager — takes goods from a listed shop without paying. In the expense form he simply picks how it was paid:

Paid by cashthe amount is cut from HIS advance balance (M-2)
On credit at [shop]nothing cut from his balance — the amount goes on the SHOP's account

Approval works exactly the same (photos, voice, category, limits). Once approved, the amount counts in the site's expense and in that shop's outstanding.

V-3 The shop's khata — settle with full history

  • Every shop's page shows: total purchased · total paid · balance owed — month by month.
  • When you or the Site Manager pay the shop, that payment is recorded → the balance comes down.
  • Month-end settlement happens from your records, not from the shopkeeper's diary.
